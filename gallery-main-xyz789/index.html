These photos were taken on August 7th 2019 at the Royal Ontario Museum in Toronto at the exhibition called 'Treasures of a Desert Kingdom. The Royal Arts of Jodhpur, India'

Elephant Howdah

The magnificent object is a royal Elephant Howdah, a ceremonial seat for riding on an elephant.Here is some more information about it:

Origin: Jodhpur, India (from the Mehrangarh Museum Trust). Date: Created in the early 19th century. Materials: It is crafted from wood which is then gilded (covered in gold leaf) and features intricate carvings, glass, and metalwork.

Purpose: This was not for everyday travel. It was used by the Maharaja (the king) for grand ceremonial processions, religious festivals, and royal weddings.

Symbolism: Its incredible opulence and detailed craftsmanship were a direct display of the wealth, power, and prestige of the Rathore dynasty that ruled the kingdom of Jodhpur. The enclosed structure also offered privacy and protection from the sun for its royal occupants.
